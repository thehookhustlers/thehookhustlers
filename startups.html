Investor Psychology

What Investors Actually Look For

Most founders pitch what they built. The best founders pitch what investors want to see. Here is the difference — based on real investor feedback from across the Indian ecosystem.

✅ Must-Haves — Non-Negotiable
👥
A coachable, committed founding team
Investors back people first, ideas second. They need to believe the team will adapt, persist and make hard calls when the original plan doesn't work.
Weighted: 35% of decision
📦
A clearly defined problem worth solving
Not "we built an app." The problem must be real, painful, and large enough to justify capital. Bonus points if you've personally lived it.
Weighted: 25% of decision
📈
Some form of traction or validation
Even early signals matter — beta users, LOIs, pilot contracts, waitlist signups. Investors don't need revenue; they need proof someone wants what you built.
Weighted: 20% of decision
🏦
A large enough addressable market (TAM)
VCs need exits. Angels need returns. If the market is too small, even a perfect execution can't generate the return they need. India's context: ₹500Cr+ TAM is a baseline.
Weighted: 15% of decision
🚫 Deal Killers — Fix Before You Pitch
💸
Asking for too much, too early
A pre-revenue startup asking for ₹5 Crore with a ₹50 Crore valuation raises red flags instantly. Know the market rate for your stage and sector.
🙈
"No competition" answers
Every investor knows every market has competition. Saying you have none signals you haven't done your research — or you don't understand your own market.
📄
No documentation or financial model
Even a basic 3-year projection shows you've thought about the business. Arriving with nothing signals unpreparedness — and that's a fast no.
